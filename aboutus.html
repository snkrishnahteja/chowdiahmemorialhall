About Us

ACADEMY OF MUSIC founded in 1961, is a premier cultural organisation dedicated to promoting performing arts. It was founded with a vision to support, encourage classical music, dance and drama and to nurture the latent talent among the youth. It has also endeavoured to create an awareness and appreciation about our rich cultural heritage vis-à-vis classical arts.

Immortal Memory to an Immortal Artist

Sangeeta Ratna Mysore T. Chowdiah is an immortal name in the world of music.

Chowdiah needs no memorial, Yet as a mark of admiration for the achievements of the seven-stringed violin maestro and in grateful remembrance of the pure joy he gave to music lovers who were mesmerised by the divine SWARAS that freely flowed from his violin.

this hall was visualised by a small group of people in the Academy of Music headed by Sri. K. K. Murthy, himself a great connoisseur and patron of pure music.

Sri. Murthy has a passion for taking on impossible tasks and finds fulfilment in making them possible. Chowdiah Memorial Hall soon took shape in the blue print. That was in the year 1976.

CHOWDIAH MEMORIAL HALL

For almost Four decade and a half now, the violin-shaped, the first of its kind anywhere in the world - The Chowdiah Memorial Hall has reverberated with the notes and beats and steps of the best of artists and the applause of enthralled, discerning music and art lovers of Bangalore.

The Hall has emerged as a national centre for Music, Dance and Drama and is equipped with state-of-art sound and lighting systems and a most modern air conditioning plant. Surgically clean surroundings, green lawns and a landscaped garden add to the charm of the unique auditorium.

A Dream

The dream of Sri. K. K. Murthy and friends found enthusiastic support from art lovers, philanthropists, socialites and politicians. All of them came forward to support the project which was taken up by the Academy of Music (founded in 1961).

Owl Creek Site

A site measuring 240' X 260' belonging to the City Corporation was available in G.D.Park Extension. It was called the Owl Creek by the local people.

We were able to secure this site through the good offices of Late Sri. B. M. Patil, the then Minister for Muncipal Administration. Sri. K. K. Murthy, the visionary, jumped at the offer and signed a 99 year lease agreement with the Corporation. People who cruise along the elevated Sankey Road (CMH Road) get a grand stand, majestic view of the imposing hall and the beautiful garden around it.

FROM A DREAM TO REALITY

The hall needed not only a ton a bricks, but tons of bricks, apart from steel, aluminium, wood, glass, marble, machinery, furniture and many many other things. The money came from the public, Syndicate Bank, Government of Karnataka, City Corporation and many more corporate bodies. The Academy is beholden to Late Sri. R. Gundu Rao, the then Chief Minister of Karnataka for promptly sanctioning a grant of Rs. 20 lakhs in the very first Cabinet Meeting chaired by him as the Chief Minister in the year 1980. The same year the Hall was inaugurated.

A COLLECTIVE EFFORT

Architects, Engineers, Contractors, experts in various fields toiled for four years. Late Sri. S. N. Murthy, Architect, designed the hall in the shape of a violin.

Thus the dream came true in the year 1980. It stands as a memorial not only to the legendary violin maestro, Sangeeta Ratna Mysore T. Chowdiah but also to the dedication and selfless service of many people behind it.
